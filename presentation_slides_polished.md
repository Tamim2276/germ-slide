# Slide 2: Study Background

- Train food services often depend on manual planning.
- Passenger demand changes by route and time.
- This creates waste, shortages, and delays.
- Similar digital tools work well in other food-service sectors.
- Railway catering still lacks a smart integrated system.

**Speaker notes:** Emphasize that the problem is operational inefficiency caused by unpredictable demand.

---

# Slide 3: Research Gap

- Most studies focus on restaurants, campuses, airlines, or supply chains.
- Existing solutions usually solve only one issue.
- Few studies connect forecasting, waste reduction, and transparency.
- Railway food services remain underexplored in the literature.

**Speaker notes:** State clearly that the gap is the absence of railway-specific, integrated research.

# Slide 4: Study Objectives

## General Objective

- To evaluate how a smart food ordering and waste reduction system can improve train food services.

## Specific Objectives

- To assess current train food service limitations.
- To compare related digital food-service studies.
- To identify useful technologies for railway catering.
- To examine effects on satisfaction, waste, and sustainability.

# Slide 6: Key Findings / Results

- Digital pre-ordering can reduce overproduction.
- Better forecasting improves meal planning.
- Passengers value convenience, quality, and reliability.
- Transparency tools increase trust in food services.
- Waste reduction also supports sustainability goals.
- Railway systems need route-based and time-sensitive planning.

**Speaker notes:** Highlight the practical insight that railway services need tailored digital support.

---

# Slide 7: Recommendations / Future Research Directions

- Develop a railway-specific mobile ordering platform.
- Include pre-ordering, tracking, and multiple payment options.
- Add QR-based food origin and freshness information.
- Test the system on selected train routes first.
- Expand future studies to more routes and larger samples.
- Examine cost, scalability, and long-term impact.

**Speaker notes:** Focus on realistic implementation and future validation.

- Train food services need a smarter ordering model.

- Transparency can improve passenger confidence.
- A mixed-method approach gives useful evidence for future design.
- The study provides a foundation for better railway food services.
