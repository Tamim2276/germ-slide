# Slide 2: Study Background

- Train food services often rely on manual planning and traditional ordering methods.
- Passenger demand is variable, which makes food preparation difficult to predict.
- This leads to overproduction, food waste, stock shortages, and poor passenger satisfaction.
- Digital food ordering, forecasting, and traceability tools have improved food services in other sectors.
- However, railway food services still lack a smart, integrated, and demand-driven solution.

---

# Slide 3: Research Gap

- Existing studies mostly focus on restaurants, campuses, airlines, or supply chains rather than train services.
- Many solutions address only one issue, such as forecasting, waste tracking, or ordering convenience.
- There is limited research that combines demand forecasting, food waste reduction, transparency, and passenger experience for railways.
- No comprehensive comparative framework was found for applying these technologies in train food services.

---

# Slide 4: Study Objectives

## General Objective

- To analyze how a smart food ordering and waste reduction system can improve train food services.

## Specific Objectives

- To examine the limitations of current train food service practices.
- To compare related studies from food service, transport, and digital ordering systems.
- To identify technology options for improving forecasting, transparency, and waste reduction.
- To evaluate how such a system may improve passenger satisfaction and sustainability.

# Slide 6: Key Findings / Results

- Smart ordering and forecasting can reduce overproduction and food waste.
- Digital pre-ordering improves service efficiency and supports better meal planning.
- Passenger satisfaction depends on food availability, quality, convenience, and trust.
- Transparency tools such as QR codes and real-time tracking can increase confidence in the service.
- Environmental impact can be reduced through better planning and lower waste generation.
- Railway services require a system designed for moving inventory, time constraints, and route-based demand.

---

# Slide 7: Recommendations / Future Research Directions

- Develop a railway-specific mobile ordering platform.
- Include pre-ordering, real-time tracking, and multiple payment options.
- Use forecasting tools to match food preparation with passenger demand.
- Add transparency features such as QR-based origin and freshness information.
- Conduct pilot testing on selected train routes before wider implementation.
- Future research should include larger samples, more routes, and longitudinal testing.
- Further studies may examine cost-benefit, scalability, and integration with railway operations.

- Train food services need a smarter and more sustainable ordering model.
